Thesis {
	I would test the word count program using english panagrams
	typed out with tabs, spaces, newlines, and capitalization.
}
example_1 {
	"The Quick	Brown Fox
	Jumps Over	the Lazy Dog"
}
example_2 {
	"Sphinx of	black quartz
	judge my	Vow"
}
Explanation {
	The use of input tested with escape sequence characters
	would most likely uncover bugs. Special characters such
	as tab (\t) and newline (\n) have to be tested separately
	from other input, so there's a greater chance for error.
	
	Additionally, panagrams with capitalization test a wide
	range of the ASCII character set, giving us a better
	chance to expose flaws in the program.
}
